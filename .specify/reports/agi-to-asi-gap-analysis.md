# From AGI to ASI — Gap Analysis vs agent-utilities

**Date:** 2026-06-13
**Paper:** Google DeepMind, "From AGI to ASI" (arXiv:2606.12683)
**Subject:** `agent-utilities` (AU) — the 5-pillar self-evolving agent architecture (EG-KG.compute.backend / ORCH-1 / AHE-3 / AU-ECO.connector.plane-provisioning-auth / OS-5, with a new SAFE-1 pillar opened here)

---

## Executive summary — the honest verdict

The paper frames the AGI→ASI transition as four (overlapping) pathways — **scaling**, **paradigm shifts**, **recursive self-improvement**, and **multi-agent collectives** — gated by a set of **frictions** (data wall, resource/energy limits, paradigm insufficiency, model collapse, "research gets harder", governance), with **Universal AI / AIXI** (a universal log-loss predictor + separate planning/search scaffolding) as the theoretical endpoint. Its recurring meta-thesis: for every friction you need **either a countermeasure or an instrumented way to measure whether the bottleneck is binding.**

Measured against that frame, AU is **unusually strong on foundations and interconnectedness, and unusually weak on closing the loops it has already half-built.** The honest verdict:

- **AU has the substrate for every pathway already deployed.** Stateless horizontal scale-out (ORCH-1.45), elastic engine residency (AU-KG.sharding.elastic-over-kg-shard), durable externalized state (AU-OS.state.unified-durable-state-externalization/17/18), reactive autoscaling (OS-5.29), test-time compute scaling (AHE-3.1/3.16, ORCH-1.1 RLM), a model-agnostic + symbolic-backend-pluggable reasoning stack (ORCH-1.27, AU-KG.domains.legal-automation), a *deployed* propose→govern→publish self-evolution daemon (AU-AHE.assimilation.research-auto-merge/3.18/3.20/3.21), a 1→300+ agent parallel engine with an explicit coordination layer and a social-system model (ORCH-1.8/1.3/1.32), and a deep deterministic eval/safety/observability plane (AHE-3.1, OS-5.24, ECO-4.40/4.41). Almost nothing here is aspirational.
- **The gaps are overwhelmingly "close the loop" gaps, not "build from scratch" gaps.** AU repeatedly *generates the right artifacts but never feeds them back*: it scales test-time compute but never harvests the search outputs into training data; it runs an evolution loop but never generates the code, never re-measures capability against a baseline, and never tracks its own improvement rate; it runs collectives but never measures whether adding agents helps. **The dominant deficiency is missing feedback edges and missing instrumentation — exactly the paper's "measure whether the bottleneck is binding" theme.**

**Top 3 highest-leverage gaps (all marked `leverage: high`, all `closeable_in_repo: true`):**

1. **AHE-3.22 — the deployed self-improvement loop never generates code.** The closed loop can promote, govern, sandbox-validate and branch a *code* change, but nothing in the live path ever emits the diff. Self-improvement bottoms out at "propose a spec / open a TeamSpec." This is the single biggest distance from the paper's **genotypic RSI**.
2. **AU-OS.scaling.kg-provenance-panel-data / AHE-3.25 — no conversion of test-time search outputs into training data (AlphaZero-style recursive distillation).** AU produces exactly the synthetic-data goldmine the paper names (diverse verified best-of-k candidates, scored RLM trajectories, golden-loop results) and throws it on the floor. This is the highest-leverage **scaling/data-wall + memetic-RSI** lever AU is leaving unused.
3. **AU-OS.scaling.kg-provenance-panel-data's sibling — the data-wall lever generally.** Past the high-quality-text wall, the paper's mechanism is search-augmented distillation + synthetic data fed back to the prior; AU has the trainer (ML-001..007) and the harvest sources but no factory between them.

The rest of the gaps are medium/low leverage **instrumentation and seam** gaps (returns-on-compute governor, world-model, Reasoner abstraction, multi-agent scaling laws, RSI velocity, model-collapse guard, corrigibility primitives). Almost all are closeable in-repo; what is genuinely *not* closeable in-repo is the foundational substrate (new architectures, true scaling-law theory at frontier scale, the hardware/energy frontier) and large-N validation runs.

## Paper in brief — the four pathways, the frictions, and the Universal-AI framing

**Theoretical endpoint (§4, AIXI / Universal AI).** Intelligence is framed as **search through model-/hypothesis-space** under a Solomonoff / low-complexity (Occam) prior: a **universal log-loss predictor PLUS separate explicit planning/search scaffolding**. The meta-point: because the winning substrate is unknown, advancing **paradigm-agnostic understanding** is what de-risks progress.

**The four pathways (§5.1–§5.4):**

1. **Scaling compute, models & data (§5.1).** A few more orders of magnitude of *effective* compute, co-scaled with vast **high-quality** data under compute-optimal (Chinchilla) regimes, plus **test-time scaling** that decouples capability from static training. Key frictions: the **data wall** (transcend via search-augmented distillation, simulation, synthetic data) and the **bitter lesson** (naive brute-force search fails in non-toy domains; gains come from better priors/heuristics that raise search *efficiency*). Naive scaling of identical instances raises throughput, not an individual's intelligence — but **can run a large collective of cheap digital workers that is collectively superintelligent.** Reducing uncertainty here requires **continual quantitative forecasting** (power-law / scaling-law models re-fit to tracked inputs).
2. **Paradigm shifts (§5.2).** Evolutions of the current paradigm (test-time scaling, **retrieval-as-working-memory**, continual learning, linear-time sequence models) vs true shifts (RL-based pretraining, **explicit world models**, neuromorphic/analog, NTM-style complexity escapes). Decisive named mechanisms: **dynamic adaptive test-time computation** (offload to external engines/simulators), **robust internal world models** (compressed manipulable environment dynamics → simulate futures, plan long horizons, counterfactual zero-shot), and the AIXI recipe. The only robust posture is **substrate-pluggability.**
3. **Recursive (self-)improvement (§5.3).** AI doing AI R&D → better AI → faster R&D, potentially **explosive** — but only if **fully autonomous over an extended capability range.** Four mechanisms: **genotypic** (better code/architectures/harnesses), **memetic** (AI-curated/synthetic data + recursive distillation of test-time search, AlphaZero-style), **sociogenic** (division of labor), **hardware**. Safety via **verified program synthesis** + iterated amplification. Dominant friction: dynamics are poorly understood → measure each mechanism, fit **recursive-improvement scaling laws**, build a theory of recursive distillation (verifier quality, cadence, degeneration), track AI-Scientist productivity.
4. **Multi-agent collectives (§5.4).** ASI as a **collective property** of coordinated AGI agents ("Group Agents" / automated corporations). Two regimes: **decentralized self-organization via Virtual Agent Economies** (price signals coordinate vast populations) vs **centralized high-bandwidth coordination** of goal-aligned copies. Central mechanism: **cognitive division of labour** (specialization + the coordination it forces). Open questions: does a homogeneous LLM collective yield synergy; how does collective intelligence scale with population size and interaction density (**Multi-Agent Scaling Laws**); which organizational form emerges where (steerable by mechanism design).

**Frictions, bottlenecks & open questions (§5.5 Table 3/4, §6 Remarks, §7 Outlook).** Data wall (+ model-collapse risk of self-generated data), economic/resource/energy/interconnect limits, paradigm insufficiency, abstraction/embodiment barriers, "research gets harder", deliberate slowdown/governance. Because ASI capability is partly **unpredictable** (Kolmogorov / lossy-compression irreducibility), progress must be tracked empirically — demanding **non-saturating superhuman benchmarks** with little human input, **multi-agent scaling laws**, **recursive-improvement instrumentation**, **compute↔capability↔economics forecasting**, and **safety/autonomy controls** (corrigibility, knowledge-seeking objectives, oracle/myopic decoupling).

### HIGH leverage

#### AHE-3.22 (§5.3 recursive — genotypic RSI) — Deployed loop is prose-proposal-only; no autonomous code-generation step fills the code path that already exists
- **Paper claim:** Genotypic RSI = AI writing better code (architectures/optimizers/harnesses) for the next system; "verified program synthesis to safely patch critical subsystems" presumes the AI actually emits the patch.
- **AU current state:** `knowledge_graph/research/change_synthesis.py` materializes code **only** when a proposal already embeds explicit `files`/`files_json` artifacts; otherwise it emits a `kind='sdd_plan'` markdown skeleton (its docstring admits "no LLM calls"). The deployed synth stage (`golden_loop.py:464 _synthesize_team` + `enrichment/synthesize.py`) produces only prose TeamSpec/AgentSpec nodes; `distillation/physical_distiller.py` (AHE-3.9) only rewrites docstrings/frontmatter "without altering any surrounding code." Nothing in the deployed loop ever populates `files`, so the entire `kind='code'` branch is **reachable but never fed.**
- **Gap:** The closed loop can promote, govern, sandbox-validate and branch a code change, but nothing in the live path **generates the diff.** A human still writes every line.
- **Leverage:** **high.** Closeable: **yes.**
- **Proposed direction:** Add a code-synthesis stage between promotion and `change_synthesis`: an RLM/agent step that, for a `failure_gap`/concept proposal whose target component is known (`failure_analyzer` already attributes `component_type` + `file_path`), reads the target file(s) and emits a `{path, content}` diff into the proposal's `files` field — reusing the existing change_synthesis→sandbox→publisher pipeline unchanged. Constrain to single-file component-attributed edits first; wire `EvolveAgent` (already proposes file-level `ComponentEdits`, has a verifier) as the generator.
- **Concept / SDD:** `AHE-3.22` → `/home/apps/workspace/agent-packages/agent-utilities/.specify/specs/ahe-3-22-deployed-loop-is-prose-proposal-only-no-autonomo`
- **Refs:** AHE-3.21, AU-AHE.harness.failure-evolution, AHE-3.0, ORCH-1.38

#### AU-OS.scaling.kg-provenance-panel-data (§5.1 scaling — data wall) — No conversion of test-time search outputs into training/distillation data
- **Paper claim:** §5.1/§5.3: the way past the high-quality-text data wall is "transcending human-generated data limits" via search-augmented distillation, simulation and synthetic data — AlphaZero-style, "distilling search outputs and self-play are forms of converting test-time compute into better data," regularly distilled back to improve the search prior.
- **AU current state:** `knowledge_graph/distillation/` (KG-2.2) dedups/merges **ingested** knowledge; the golden loop (`research/golden_loop.py`, KG-2.7/2.10) is explicitly **propose-only** ("No code execution, no PR merge"). AU-AHE.harness.width-diverse-best-k (`graph/test_time_diversity.py`) produces diverse best-of-k candidates at inference but **discards** winning/losing trajectories. RLM RunTraces (ORCH-1.29) are kept for GEPA prompt reflection only. `harness/preference_pairs.py` reads only eval_case/preference/correction nodes — never best-of-k winners.
- **Gap:** AU generates exactly the artifacts the paper calls the synthetic-data goldmine (diverse verified candidate sets, scored RLM trajectories, golden-loop results) but **never harvests them** into a curated trainable corpus (rejection-sampled best trajectories, preference pairs, distillation targets). No self-play/search-distillation factory feeds the existing trainer (ML-001..007, data-science-mcp). **Highest-leverage scaling lever AU leaves on the floor.**
- **Leverage:** **high.** Closeable: **yes.**
- **Proposed direction:** Build a `SearchDistillation` harvester: tap AU-AHE.harness.width-diverse-best-k candidate sets + `harness/verifier.py` scores + RLM RunTraces, apply rejection sampling/best-of-k to mint `(prompt → best-trajectory)` SFT rows and `(winner, loser)` preference pairs, dedup via the KG-2.2 LSH/deduplicator pipeline, persist a versioned synthetic corpus the data-science-mcp trainer (ML-001..007) consumes. Run it as a new gated `distil-to-data` golden-loop stage. Pure in-repo wiring.

#### AHE-3.25 (§5.3 recursive — memetic RSI) — No recursive distillation of test-time search back into a prior (AlphaZero-style)
- **Paper claim:** Memetic RSI / agenda 4d: convert test-time compute into better training data by distilling search-improved outputs back into the search-prior; asks for distillation-cadence / base-model-size / degeneration trade-offs.
- **AU current state:** AU has both halves but no loop: test-time fan-out + best-of-k (AU-AHE.harness.width-diverse-best-k), preference-pair mining (AU-AHE.harness.preference-corpus-reliability `preference_pairs.py`), reward-EMA capability router write-back (`capability_index.record_outcome` — only a routing EMA, not a generation prior), and a from-scratch/fine-tune trainer (ML-001..007). `optimization_engine.py:1744` explicitly marks the fine-tune stage "out of scope." Log distillation cadence vs capability delta to empirically probe agenda-4d degeneration on AU's own loop.
- **Concept / SDD:** `AHE-3.25` → `/home/apps/workspace/agent-packages/agent-utilities/.specify/specs/ahe-3-25-no-recursive-distillation-of-test-time-search-ba`

- **Paper claim:** §5.1: sustaining scaling = co-scaling compute/data/cost in compute-optimal regimes; the digital-worker-collective lever depends on running workers "orders of magnitude faster thanks to more compute and more compute efficiency" — place work on the cheapest/fastest compute, not just add replicas anywhere.

## What AU already has — strong foundations & interconnectedness

The case for AU's strength is not that it has one or two impressive modules — it is that **the substrate for all four pathways is already deployed and wired into each other.** Synthesizing the verified strengths across pathways:

**Scaling substrate (the digital-worker-collective lever).** AU's agent tier is already a stateless, horizontally scalable fleet, not an aspiration: ORCH-1.45 externalizes agent-turn dispatch onto a durable session-partitioned queue (`orchestration/agent_dispatch.py` + `agent_dispatch_worker.py`) so sessions are no longer pinned to their birth host; AU-KG.sharding.elastic-over-kg-shard (`tenant_engine_pool.py`) makes "thousands of tenants in one process" tractable with a bounded warm LRU pool + hydrate-on-miss + non-lossy L3 eviction; AU-OS.state.unified-durable-state-externalization/5.17/5.18 externalize all state to shared Postgres (one `STATE_DB_URI`), do cross-host singleton leadership via PG advisory locks, and expose a SQL-aggregated fleet plane; OS-5.29 (`fleet_autoscaler.py` + `scaling_signals.py`) closes a reactive autoscaling loop through the OS-5.24 ActionPolicy gate. Test-time compute is first-class (AHE-3.1 continuous effort, AU-AHE.harness.width-diverse-best-k VPO diverse best-of-k, ORCH-1.1 RLM decomposition + ORCH-1.38 tiered sandbox), and cost is centrally priced (ECO-4.40) with a confidence-gated router (ORCH-1.2).

## Open research questions the paper raises that AU cannot close alone

AU can build the *measurement* and *engineering* half of almost everything above. It cannot, by itself, resolve the genuinely foundational questions — and honesty about ASI-scale limits matters:

- **The substrate itself.** New architectures / optimisation procedures (RL-based pretraining, neuromorphic/analog compute, NTM-style complexity escapes, linear-time sequence models, a *learned parametric* world model) are foundational-model research. AU can stay substrate-pluggable (KG-2.64/2.65/2.66 make the seams) but cannot invent the new paradigm.
- **True scaling-law theory at the frontier.** AU can fit *its own local* curves (multi-agent scaling AU-ORCH.execution.robust-multi-format-edit/AU-OS.scaling.multi-agent-scaling-law, recursive-improvement AU-AHE.sdd.recursive-improvement-instrumentation-aggregating/AU-OS.audit.recursive-improvement-velocity-tracker, compute-forecasting), but the paper's call for scaling laws that *forecast ASI* requires runs orders of magnitude beyond a homelab — large-N collectives and base-model fine-tunes both **need external compute**. AU becomes one empirical datapoint, not the law.
- **The data wall at the limit.** AU can convert test-time search into training data (AU-OS.scaling.kg-provenance-panel-data/AHE-3.25) and guard the corpus against collapse (AU-OS.safety.model-collapse-guard-self), but whether self-generated/synthetic data can *durably* substitute for the exhausting supply of high-quality human text — and the exact degeneration thresholds (agenda 4d) — is an open empirical question AU can probe but not settle.
- **Hardware / energy / interconnect frontier.** AU can *account* for FLOPs/joules-per-capability (the measurement half), but the bandwidth/memory/energy bottlenecks themselves (§7.2) are physical and external.
- **The unpredictability floor.** §6/§7 ground ASI capability in Kolmogorov / lossy-compression irreducibility — some superhuman capability is *not* predictable from below in principle. AU's best response is exactly what it is missing today: relentless empirical instrumentation (non-saturating benchmarks SAFE-1.1, RSI velocity, multi-agent scaling) so progress is *tracked* even where it cannot be *forecast*. The verdict's most important implication: **AU's biggest ASI-relevant deficit is not capability, it is that it does not yet measure its own dynamics — and that is the one thing it can fix entirely in-repo.**
- **Deep safety theory.** Corrigibility, knowledge-seeking objectives robust to the Delusion Box, oracle/myopic decoupling (SAFE-1.5) — AU can ship the *engineering primitives*, but the formal guarantees and the alignment theory behind them remain open research.
